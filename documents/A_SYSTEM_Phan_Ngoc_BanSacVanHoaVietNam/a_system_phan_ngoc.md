## RCA status labels (LLM-friendly)

The `RCA status` column is a reliability-and-provenance label for each concept or edge:

- `core synthesis`: synthesized from source evidence into the compact core system.
- `direct`: explicitly supported by cited source passages.
- `implied`: inferred from the source framework; use as a cautious structural relation, not a direct claim.
- `synthesized`: assembled from multiple scattered passages that together constitute a coherent account.
- `listed only`: term appears in the source but is not elaborated enough for expansion.
- `OCR-normalized`: spelling was normalized from OCR/source noise; preserve the normalized form but verify if critical.
- `meta`: describes the work's method or structure, not doctrinal concepts.
- `—`: no special RCA qualifier beyond the cited source line.
