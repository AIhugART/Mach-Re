## PART R1 — FINDINGS

### F1 — C1 contradiction: the axiom's primary evidence violates the axiom's stated activation condition

**Round 1 — Symptom.**
- v3.1 Part 5 Step 4: "Axiom IX activates only when all four conditions
  are met"; C1: "Both S1 and S2 must be operating reflexive cognition."
- v3.1 Part 7 stack: "Prerequisite: Axiom VIII active in **at least one
  system**."
- v3.1 Part 6 primary case: Vietnam sustains a Living Interface while
  partner systems have "intermittent VIII" — explicitly called "the
  strongest evidence for Axiom IX."
Three statements; at most one can be canonical. Under the strict Part 5
reading, the primary Vietnamese Verification Case would *falsify* C1
rather than verify it.

**Round 2 — Mechanism.** A framework whose meta-rule is internal
consistency (Axiom VIII applied to itself) is damaged most by shipping an
axiom whose strongest VVC contradicts the axiom's own activation clause.
Any careful reader — exactly the reader Mạch Rễ invites — will find this
in minutes. Compass B confirms the graded reading is the structurally
correct one: by Requisite Variety [2], the system with greater *reflexive
variety* (stronger VIII) can absorb the disturbances of the interface that
the weaker partner cannot, so an asymmetric-but-open interface is exactly
what cybernetics predicts — it is the strict symmetric clause that was
wrong, not the evidence.

**Round 3 — Root (one sentence).** v3.1 added the Vietnam-primary case
(empirically asymmetric C1) on top of v3.0's symmetric formal clause while
declaring "nothing in the axiom content of v3.0 is retracted," so the
activation condition was frozen even though the new primary evidence
required re-deriving it.

| Criterion | Score | Reasoning |
|---|---|---|
| Correct | 5/5 | Direct textual contradiction across Parts 5 / 6 / 7 |
| Deep | 5/5 | Root is the "architectural-only" freeze, not wording |
| Feasible | 4/5 | Touches the formal text, but as an additive specification (graded modes), no retraction |
| Conflict-risk | 4/5 | Resolves rather than creates conflict; Open Question 1 must be updated in sync |
| Preservation | 5/5 | Keeps both the formal claim and the primary VVC by giving each its mode |

**Average 4.6/5 → FIX.** Amendment V1.

### F5 — Absolute / unverified empirical claims in the VVCs

**Round 1 — Symptom.** Four instances:
1. "faster than any comparable former-enemy pair in the 20th century" /
   "has no parallel" — absolute, and prima facie counterexamples exist
   (US–Japan 1945→1951, US–West Germany 1945→1955), escaped only by the
   undefined word "comparable" → unfalsifiable as worded.
2. "4,000 years" stated as historical fact — it is a cultural
   self-description ("bốn nghìn năm văn hiến"), contested as
   historiography.
3. "Attributed to Huỳnh Thúc Kháng, deployed by Hồ Chí Minh" — standard
   accounts run the other way: Hồ Chí Minh spoke "Dĩ bất biến, ứng vạn
   biến" *to* Huỳnh Thúc Kháng when entrusting him the acting presidency
   (May 1946); the phrase itself has a classical Chinese lineage
   (以不變應萬變). Attribution as printed is likely reversed and is
   unsourced either way.
4. "required 22 years from the end of direct military conflict" — the
   baseline (1973 Paris Accords vs 1975) is never stated.

**Round 2 — Mechanism.** `dictionary_rule.md` §1 bans exactly this class
("không có tiền lệ" → "ít có tiền lệ so sánh trực tiếp") and §3 requires
history to *illustrate*, never *prove*. A framework that advertises
falsifiability is hurt most where it makes its proudest empirical claim
in absolute form. Compass C: Pramāṇa — a deceptive (unsourced, absolute)
statement is invalid cognition regardless of whether it is approximately
true; Two Truths — "fastest ever" elevates a conventional comparison to
ultimate status.

**Round 3 — Root (one sentence).** The VVCs were written in narrative
register and never passed through the §1 absolute→scoped substitution
table or the Claim Ladder, because the EAP (which mandates VVCs) was
created in the same document and no checklist yet says "VVC prose obeys
dictionary_rule §1–§5."

Scores: Correct 5, Deep 4, Feasible 5, Conflict-risk 4, Preservation 4
(the anchor stays; only its armor changes).
**Average 4.4/5 → FIX.** Amendment V2.

### V1 — Graded condition C1 (overrides Part 5 Step 4 C1 and Part 7 stack line; updates Open Question 1)

**C1 — REFLEXIVE COGNITION IN THE CONTACT ZONE (graded, two modes)**

- **C1-full (symmetric mode):** both S1 and S2 are running Axiom VIII.
  The Living Interface is stable; P* is co-maintained. This is the
  condition the formal statement of Axiom IX describes ("each operating
  Axioms I–VIII").
- **C1-min (asymmetric mode):** at least one system runs Axiom VIII
  consistently and with sufficient strength to hold the contact zone
  open while partner VIII is intermittent or constrained. The Living
  Interface exists but is **degraded**: maintenance cost concentrates on
  the stronger-VIII system, and P* stability depends on that system's
  continued reflexive operation. Structural grounding (compass B):
  by Requisite Variety [2], the system with greater reflexive variety
  can regulate interface disturbances the weaker partner cannot.
- **C1-zero:** no system runs VIII → no Living Interface. The contact
  zone defaults to battleground or neutralization (unchanged from v3.1).

**Boundary against Axiom IV (resolves the open part of Open Question 1):**
asymmetric C1-min is still Axiom IX — not "Axiom IV with a strong
absorber" — *if and only if* the weaker-VIII system retains its own
invariant pattern (its Axiom II keeps functioning and it is not being
restructured by the stronger system's pattern logic). If the weaker
system's invariant is being overwritten, the situation is Axiom IV
absorption, not a Living Interface.

**Vietnamese Verification Case (unchanged, now consistent):** Bamboo
Diplomacy is the canonical VVC of **C1-min** — Vietnam holding multiple
interfaces open with partners whose VIII is intermittent. Trúc Lâm
(both systems rooted and reflexive) is the canonical VVC of **C1-full**.

**Version honesty note:** this is a substantive specification (it changes
what C1 requires), not a presentational fix. Open Question 1 is narrowed
accordingly: what remains open is only the *quantitative* threshold of
"sufficient strength", not the qualitative boundary.
