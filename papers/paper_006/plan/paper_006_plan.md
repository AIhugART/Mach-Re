## 0. MỤC TIÊU PAPER

### 0.1 Vấn đề (Problem Statement)

Philosophical frameworks that claim cultural grounding face a traceability gap: their axioms may be logically coherent and triangulated with established theory (Western + Eastern), but lack **inductive empirical instances** from the culture they claim to describe. Without such instances, the cultural claim remains an assertion, not a demonstrated alignment.

Mạch Rễ (Root-Circuit), a relational-distributed philosophical framework, exemplifies this gap. Its 8 axioms were derived from Phan Ngọc's cultural theory (Anchor A), Ashby/Weick cybernetics (Anchor B), and Buddhist epistemology (Anchor C) — but Anchor A lacked Vietnamese empirical instances from the very cultural corpus the framework claims to ground itself in.

### 0.2 Câu hỏi nghiên cứu (Research Question)

> **Can Vietnamese folk epistemology — specifically the ca dao (folk poetry) and proverb corpus — provide systematic inductive evidence for a relational-distributed philosophical framework, and what method makes this mapping rigorous rather than anecdotal?**

### 0.3 Luận điểm trung tâm (Thesis)

We present **"evidence enrichment"** — a method for strengthening a philosophical framework's cultural triangulation anchor (Anchor A) by systematically mapping a folk epistemology corpus onto the framework's axioms. The method treats each axiom as a mapping target, each corpus entry as a candidate empirical instance, and evaluates each mapping through a 3-round Root Cause Analysis (RCA) gate with a scoring threshold (≥ 4/5). Applied to Mạch Rễ and the Vietnamese ca dao corpus (~18,000+ entries across 4 sources: Q1, Q2, Q3, Nam An), the method produced 6 evidence files with 15 mapping claims (RCA 4.6–5.0/5) across all 8 axioms, revealing (a) structural convergence between folk epistemology and formal axioms at a level beyond chance, (b) 5 evidence gaps invisible to traditional content-theme mapping methods, and (c) two independent convergent discoveries (Lévi-Strauss→Axiom IV, folk wisdom→Root Question R) that strengthen the framework without proving it.

## 1. LAYER E — EPISTEMIC LAYER

### E.1 — Five-Layer RCA Stack

```text
Phenomenon (L1):
Philosophical frameworks claiming cultural grounding often lack inductive
empirical instances from that culture's own epistemic corpus (folk poetry,
proverbs, oral tradition). The cultural claim risks being an assertion rather
than a demonstrated alignment.

Proximate Cause (L2):
Framework designers derive axioms from established theory (Western philosophy,
cybernetics, Buddhist epistemology) but do not systematically search a folk
corpus for convergent patterns — either because folk material is dismissed
as "non-philosophical," or because no method exists for rigorous cross-level
mapping between folk epistemology and formal axioms.

Underlying Mechanism (L3):
The epistemic gap is not about content quantity but about method. Folk material
operates at a different analytical level (content/expression) than formal axioms
(mechanism/structure). Mapping between these levels requires: (a) treating the
folk corpus as a distributed epistemic system, not a collection of anecdotes;
(b) applying a consistent evaluation gate (RCA) to every mapping claim;
(c) distinguishing structural coverage ("axiom explains this domain") from
empirical evidence ("this domain provides instances for the axiom").

Root Cause / New Principle (L4):
The root methodological problem is the conflation of two distinct questions:
(1) "Does the framework structurally cover this domain?" and
(2) "Does this domain provide empirical instances for the framework?"
A method that systematically separates these two questions — and
operationalizes the second through corpus-wide mapping + RCA gate —
turns folk epistemology from decorative illustration into inductive evidence.

Generalization (L5):
The "evidence enrichment" method applies to any philosophical framework that:
(a) has explicitly stated axioms with falsification conditions;
(b) claims grounding in a specific culture; and
(c) has access to a folk epistemology corpus (oral poetry, proverbs,
origin myths, place-name taxonomies). It does not apply to frameworks
without cultural grounding claims, or to corpora too small for pattern
detection (< ~100 entries).
```

### E.2 — Three-Why Drill (main claim)

```text
Claim:
Folk epistemology can serve as a systematic inductive anchor for a relational-
distributed philosophical framework when mapped through a rigorous evidence
enrichment method with RCA gate evaluation.

Symptom:
Mạch Rễ's Anchor A (Vietnamese cultural theory) scored only ~2.5/3 on
triangulation due to lack of empirical instances from Vietnamese folk material.

Why 1:
Ex ante, no method existed for systematically mapping a folk corpus onto
formal philosophical axioms — existing approaches either cherry-pick
illustrative quotes (decorative) or dismiss folk material entirely.

Why 2:
The folk corpus operates at content level (what people express) while axioms
operate at mechanism level (why systems persist). Without a level-bridging
method, mapping is either forced (claiming equivalence where only analogy
exists) or avoided entirely.

Why 3:
The underlying assumption blocking method development is that folk epistemology
is "pre-philosophical" — a collection of sentiments, not a structured epistemic
system. Reversing this assumption: the ca dao corpus IS a distributed epistemic
system (organized by themes, transmitted orally, stabilized by invariant patterns).
Once treated as a system, it becomes mappable to another system (axioms).

Root Cause:
The methodological gap is a category error: treating folk epistemology as
"content to be illustrated" rather than as "evidence to be mapped against
structural claims."

Fix:
Define "evidence enrichment" as a method with explicit steps: (1) corpus scope
identification, (2) axiom-by-axiom mapping with RCA gate, (3) triangulation
score update tracking, (4) post-execution audit against original coverage
analysis. Operate within strict constraint: NEVER modify axiom statements;
only add prior-art annotations.

Boundary:
This paper does NOT claim that folk epistemology proves Mạch Rễ correct.
It claims that the evidence enrichment method produces systematic, auditable
inductive support that strengthens the framework's cultural triangulation
anchor beyond the anecdotal level.

Verification:
6 evidence files with 15 mapping claims, all through 3-round RCA gate
(scoring ≥ 4/5), producing 10 prior-art annotations in axiom_spec.md
without modifying any axiom statement.
```

## 2. LAYER S — STRUCTURAL LAYER

### S.1 — Open with Contradiction

**Paragraph 1 — Phenomenon:**
Vietnamese folk poetry (ca dao) contains ~18,000+ entries spanning 14 life-domains, organized by professional folklorists since 1969. It is one of the world's largest systematically documented oral poetry corpora — yet it has never been used as inductive evidence for a philosophical framework.

**Paragraph 2 — Prior expectation:**
Philosophical frameworks that claim Vietnamese cultural grounding (Mạch Rễ being one) should be able to demonstrate alignment with Vietnamese folk epistemology. The default expectation is that such alignment would require cherry-picking — selecting a few convenient quotes while ignoring the vast majority of the corpus.

**Paragraph 3 — Contradiction:**
When the entire ca dao corpus is systematically mapped against Mạch Rễ's 8 axioms through a rigorous RCA gate, the alignment is not random or cherry-picked: 15 mapping claims across all 8 axioms survive the gate (RCA ≥ 4/5), 3 folk themes are correctly identified as outside scope (individual psychology), and — most surprisingly — the mapping reveals 5 evidence gaps that content-theme analysis had missed, plus 2 independent convergent discoveries where folk epistemology and formal theory independently state the same structural principle.

**Paragraph 4 — Approach:**
This paper presents "evidence enrichment" — the method that produced this mapping — as a replicable approach for any framework that claims cultural grounding and has access to a folk epistemology corpus.

- Language barrier: ca dao in Vietnamese, framework in English — translation risk
- What the method CANNOT do: prove axioms, replace derivations, guarantee acceptance

- Next step: independent application by a different researcher on a different framework

**§10 — Conclusion (~300 words)**
- Restate contribution: method, not proof
- What changed in Mạch Rễ: 10 annotations, triangulation anchor strengthened
- Open question: can this method become a standard practice for culturally-grounded frameworks?

### P.2 — Abstract Formula (5-sentence)

1. **Anchor:** Philosophical frameworks claiming cultural grounding face a traceability gap — their axioms may lack inductive instances from the culture's own epistemic corpus.
2. **Gap:** Folk epistemology (oral poetry, proverbs, origin myths) is typically treated as illustration, not as systematic evidence — no method exists for rigorous cross-level mapping.
3. **Extend:** We present "evidence enrichment," a 4-step method that treats a folk corpus as a distributed epistemic system and maps it onto axioms through a 3-round RCA evaluation gate (scoring threshold ≥ 4/5).
4. **Case:** Applied to Mạch Rễ (8 axioms) and the Vietnamese ca dao corpus (~18,000 entries, 4 sources), the method produced 15 mapping claims (RCA 4.6–5.0/5) across all 8 axioms, revealed 5 evidence gaps, and uncovered 2 independent convergent discoveries.
5. **Conclusion:** Evidence enrichment strengthens a framework's cultural triangulation anchor from anecdotal to systematic — without modifying axiom statements or claiming empirical proof.
