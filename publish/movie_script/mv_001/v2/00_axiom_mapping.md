| Axiom | Role in D11 | How it operates in the story |
|-------|------------|------------------------------|
| **III** (Orthogonal Temporality) | V-axis exists — but the recording interface (genealogy book) systematically excludes women from it | The genealogy book IS the V-axis interface. Women exist in V (they birth, nurture, transmit) but are recorded as "wife of…" / "mother of…" — never as nodes. |
| **IV** (Dynamic Boundary) | Boundary closed to women → violates selective permeability → becomes a wall for gender | The genealogy book's boundary is selectively permeable for men (all sons recorded), impermeable for women (no daughters recorded). This is a boundary pathology. |

| **VIII** (Reflexive Cognition) | System never re-examines invariant "only men are recorded" → invariant becomes dogma | For hundreds of years, no one asked "why aren't women recorded?" ## 2. Buddhist Epistemology Compass (BE-1 to BE-5)

### Why Buddhist Epistemology as Compass?

The central question of the film is ontological AND epistemological: "Does my daughter exist in this lineage?" The answer depends on **who records**, **through what interface**, **at what level of truth**. Buddhist epistemology provides the categories to distinguish these levels.

The screenplay must operationalize these categories as **structure** (not theme, not dialogue). No character ever explains Buddhist epistemology. The categories work UNDER the drama — they are the invisible architecture.

### BE-1: Pratītyasamutpāda (Dependent Origination / Duyên Khởi)

**Core claim:** Identity arises from conditions — it has no independent existence. Nothing exists "from its own side."

**Applied to the story:** A daughter's identity in the lineage is constituted by the **act of recording** her name. When the recording interface refuses to record her, her identity IN THE LINEAGE does not arise. She exists biologically, socially, emotionally — but not in the formal structure of descent. The parallel book creates NEW conditions from which identity CAN arise.

**Key scenes:** B (Inciting — the conditions are denied), C (Midpoint — new conditions are created), E (Climax — identity arises from the new conditions)

**Falsification:** If the story implies the daughter "was always there and just needed to be recognized" → BE-1 fails. Identity in the lineage is NOT pre-existing — it is DEPENDENTLY ARISEN from the act of recording. Before recording, she does not exist in that structure.

**Dramatic consequence:** This is why the parallel book is not "recognition" — it is CREATION. Linh is not discovering something that was hidden. She is bringing something into existence that did not exist before.

### BE-2: Anattā (Non-Self / Vô Ngã)

**Core claim:** No entity has svabhāva (inherent existence / self-nature). What we call "the lineage" is a conceptual designation on a collection of relations, not a thing with its own essence.

**Applied to the story:** "The lineage" has no svabhāva — it is constituted by the recording practice. The genealogy book appears to RECORD the lineage, but in fact it CONSTITUTES it. Changing the recording changes the lineage — not because the lineage was always fluid, but because the lineage never had svabhāva to begin with.

**Key scenes:** C (Midpoint — writing Bà Cúc's name while she lives = declaring the lineage has no fixed essence), E (Climax — Bé An writing her own name = proving the lineage is empty of svabhāva)

**Falsification:** If the story treats the "old lineage" as having real essence and the "new lineage" as merely symbolic → BE-2 fails. Both are equally real (or equally empty). The difference is not ontological status but **causal efficacy** — which lineage actually determines inheritance, obligation, memory.

**Dramatic consequence:** The parallel book is not a "symbolic protest." It has the SAME ontological status as the official book. Both are saṃvṛtisat (conventional truth) — see BE-4.

### BE-3: Śūnyatā (Emptiness / Tính Không)

**Core claim:** All phenomena are empty of inherent existence AND full of dependent designation. Emptiness is not nothingness — it is the absence of svabhāva combined with the presence of conventional function.

**Applied to the story:** The genealogy book is ŚŪNYA — empty of inherent "lineage-ness" and full of conventional designation (names, relations, recording acts). The official book appears FULL (filled with names) but is actually EMPTY (of women). The parallel book makes this emptiness VISIBLE — by showing what was excluded, it reveals that the official book's "fullness" was always a selective designation, never a complete truth.

**Key scenes:** A (Opening — the official book appears "full" but is śūnya of women), F (Closing — the parallel book makes visible what was śūnya in the official book)

**Falsification:** If the closing image implies the parallel book is "now complete" → BE-3 fails. The parallel book is ALSO śūnya — full of the names it contains, empty of the names not yet found (Cô Tám's grandmother: "[CHƯA TÌM ĐƯỢC — HỎI TIẾP]").

### BE-4: Saṃvṛtisat / Paramārtha-sat (Two Truths / Nhị Đế)

**Core claim:** There are two levels of truth. Conventional truth (saṃvṛtisat) = what has causal efficacy in the operational domain (names, records, inheritance rights). Ultimate truth (paramārtha-sat) = the emptiness of all phenomena, including conventional truths. Confusing the two = fundamental error.

**Applied to the story:** The genealogy book is SAṂVṚTISAT — it has real causal efficacy (determines who inherits, who performs ancestor rites, who is remembered). BUT it is NOT paramārtha-sat — it does not capture the ultimate truth about "who belongs to this lineage."

**THE CENTRAL MECHANISM OF THE SYSTEM:** The system's power comes from **confusing saṃvṛtisat with paramārtha-sat**. "Xưa giờ vậy" (it's always been this way) is the mechanism that elevates a conventional recording practice to the status of ultimate truth. The parallel book EXPOSES this confusion — by creating an ALTERNATIVE saṃvṛtisat (another recording with causal efficacy), it demonstrates that the official book was never paramārtha-sat.

**This is BRIDGE-II-III applied to the screenplay:**
- Pattern (Tiên Đề II) = saṃvṛtisat: the invariant "who gets recorded" has causal efficacy in the operational domain, loses identity when the collective dissolves
- Vertical axis (Tiên Đề III) = transcendental condition: V ⊥ H is not saṃvṛtisat OR paramārtha-sat — it is the STRUCTURAL PREREQUISITE for any pattern persistence across generations
- The system's error = treating saṃvṛtisat (conventional recording practice) as if it were the transcendental condition itself (as if V ⊥ H REQUIRES patrilineal recording)

**Key scenes:** B (Inciting — "Xưa giờ vậy" = saṃvṛtisat elevated to paramārtha-sat), D (Turning Point — Bà Cúc TOUCHES her mother's name: the conventional truth BECOMES VISIBLE to her for the first time, and she sees it AS conventional truth, not ultimate truth), F (Closing — the parallel book is presented as saṃvṛtisat: a physical object on a desk, with causal efficacy, not ultimate truth)

**Falsification:** If the film ever implies the parallel book is "the truth" (paramārtha-sat) → BE-4 fails. The parallel book is ANOTHER conventional truth — it has causal efficacy, it changes things, but it is not ultimate truth. It is also śūnya.

**Dramatic consequence:** The parallel book does not REPLACE the official book. It exists ALONGSIDE it. Two conventional truths, both with causal efficacy, neither claiming ultimacy. This is why the ending is acceptance, not victory.

### BE-5: Saṃskṛta-duḥkha (Conditioned Suffering / Hành Khổ)

**Core claim:** Suffering that arises from conditions (saṃskṛta), not from anyone's malicious action. It is structural, not personal. No one causes it; everyone participates in its conditions.

**Applied to the story:** Bà Cúc's suffering is SAṂSKṚTA-DUḤKHA. No one CAUSED her to be invisible for 85 years. The conditions (patrilineal recording, "xưa giờ vậy," the silence of women) produced her invisibility. No villain. No one to blame. This is what makes the loss tragic rather than melodramatic.

**Key scenes:** D (Turning Point — 85 years of invisibility cannot be "fixed"; Bà Cúc's tears are saṃskṛta-duḥkha made visible), Act 2B (Scene 7 — the pressure to have a son is saṃskṛta-duḥkha: no one is evil, everyone is participating in conditions that produce suffering)

**Falsification:** If the film implies anyone is "to blame" for Bà Cúc's suffering → BE-5 fails. The system produces suffering, but the system is not a person — it is a set of conditions maintained by everyone, including the victims.

**Dramatic consequence:** This is WHY "loss without a villain" works structurally. It's not a stylistic choice — it's a Buddhist epistemological commitment. Saṃskṛta-duḥkha cannot be defeated by defeating a person. It can only be transformed by changing conditions — which is exactly what the parallel book does.

## 4. BRIDGE-II-III in Screenplay Terms

`axioms.html` L169-176 defines the ontology of pattern (II) and vertical axis (III):

> Pattern (II) is saṃvṛtisat: has causal efficacy in the operational domain, loses identity when the collective dissolves — not paramārtha-sat.
> Vertical axis V ⊥ H (III) does not claim svabhāva — it is a transcendental condition: the structural prerequisite for pattern persistence across generations.

### Applied to "TÊN CON":

| Ontological Level | What it is in the story | Example |
|-------------------|------------------------|---------|
| **saṃvṛtisat (conventional truth)** | The genealogy book — a recording practice with causal efficacy (determines inheritance, ritual roles, social memory) | "Con gái không ghi" — this statement has real causal power. It changes what Linh can do, what her daughter can inherit, how the family treats them. |
| **saṃvṛtisat (alternative)** | The parallel book — another recording practice, ALSO with causal efficacy | "LIÊN — mẹ của Hằng" — this statement ALSO has causal power. Chị Hằng cries. Bà Cúc sees her mother's name. The family begins to change. |
| **Transcendental condition** | V ⊥ H — the structural fact that vertical-axis persistence matters for identity. This is what makes BOTH books possible — and what makes the exclusion of women MATTER. | If V ⊥ H did not exist (if the vertical axis were irrelevant to identity), the exclusion of women from the genealogy book would be a trivia fact, not a wound. The wound EXISTS because V ⊥ H exists. |
| **NOT paramārtha-sat** | Neither book is ultimate truth. Both are conventional designations, both are śūnya, both have causal efficacy without claiming ultimacy. | The parallel book is not "the truth." The official book is not "the lie." Both are saṃvṛtisat — conventional recording practices. The distinction is not truth vs. falsehood but INCLUSION vs. EXCLUSION. |

### The System's Error (structural, not moral):

The patrilineal genealogy system commits a **category error**: it treats a conventional recording practice (saṃvṛtisat: "we record names this way") as if it were the transcendental condition itself (as if V ⊥ H REQUIRES patrilineal recording). This is the structural equivalent of "Xưa giờ vậy" — the confusion of "this is how we've always done it" with "this is how it must be done."

The parallel book corrects this error — not by claiming to be paramārtha-sat (it doesn't), but by demonstrating that ANOTHER saṃvṛtisat is possible. V ⊥ H does not require patrilineal recording. The transcendental condition is neutral to recording practice.

## 5. Falsification Conditions (Axiom-Level)

Unlike v1's scene-level falsifications, v2 defines falsification per axiom. A scene fails if it violates its primary axiom's falsification condition.

| Axiom | Falsification Condition | Applied to Screenplay |
|-------|------------------------|----------------------|
| **I** | Fail if identity survives long-term via fixed substance-core, independent of all relations | Fail if the film suggests "the daughter was always part of the lineage and just needed to be recognized" — her identity in the lineage is constituted BY the recording relation, not pre-existing |
| **II** | Fail if identity survives without any invariant pattern | Fail if the film cannot answer: "what pattern changed?" The pattern is "who gets recorded." It changed from "men only" to "men + women (in parallel)" |
| **III** | Fail if a system maintains structural invariance through major perturbations using ONLY H-axis continuity, without V-axis anchoring; OR if all "V-axis connections" reduce to H-axis memory/records | Fail if the parallel book is merely "symbolic" (H-axis record) without creating real V-axis obligations. Passes because: the parallel book DOES create real obligations — chị Hằng cries, Bà Cúc sees her mother, Bé An inherits the right to write |
| **IV** | Fail if identity survives with completely closed or completely open boundary | Fail if the official genealogy system is portrayed as intentionally evil (closed boundary as malice rather than inertia). Passes because: system operates as weather — "xưa giờ vậy," not "we hate women" |
| **V** | (Corollary of I+II — distributed = resilient) | Fail if the parallel book is controlled by a single person. Passes because: multiple women write in it (Linh, chị Hằng, Bé An) |
| **VI** | (Derived from II+III+IV) | Fail if the perturbation (daughter's birth) does not lead to pattern reorganization. Passes because: 7 years of work → parallel book → family begins to change |
| **VII** | (Derived from I+II+III+IV) | Fail if the parallel book is ordered by a central authority. Passes because: Linh starts it, but it emerges from distributed actions (each woman telling her mother's name) |
| **VIII** | Fail if a system survives fundamental change pressure across generations without any invariant-self-examination mechanism | Fail if no character from WITHIN the system questions it. **GAP in v1:** Tuấn begins to question (Scene 9), but his question doesn't become ACTION. v2 must address this. |
