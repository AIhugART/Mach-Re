## 2. Three-Round RCA — Verification Argument

### R1 — Does Japan show a V-axis operating as structural (not merely cultural)?

**Finding: YES — ie (家) system demonstrates V-axis ontological status**

```
W1: What is the V-axis claim? → V-axis = transgenerational continuity axis;
    identity persists through time across individual members.
W2: Is this present in Japan? → The ie (household) in Nakane (1970, Ch.1–2) is
    NOT the living members but the house persisting through time. Current members
    are stewards, not owners. The ie precedes and outlives any individual.
W3: Is this structural (not just cultural ritual)? → Organizational test: when ALL
    living members of the ie die or leave, the ie still exists as an obligation
    to be inherited. The unit of identity is frame-transgenerational, not
    person-contemporaneous.
W4: Is this different from Vietnamese ancestor worship? → Yes — Vietnamese thờ
    cúng tổ tiên is a practice (H-axis people performing V-axis obligation);
    Japanese ie is the structural unit itself. Different instantiation, same
    V-axis structure.
W5 (Root): V-axis in Japan is ontological — the frame (ba) is the primary unit
    of identity and it persists across time independently of its H-axis members.
```

### R2 — Does Japan show an H-axis operating independently of V-axis?

**Finding: YES — Zen, mono no aware, amae demonstrate H-axis independence**

```
W1: What is the H-axis claim? → H-axis = present-moment relations; identity in
    the current instant, not carrying V-axis obligation.
W2: Is this present in Japan? → Zen Buddhism (禅): present-moment awareness as
    primary epistemic practice. Ichi-go ichi-e (一期一会): each encounter unique
    and unrepeatable in the present. These are H-axis structures.
W3: Are these independent of V-axis? → Doi (1973): amae (甘え) = present-moment
    emotional dependency — operates in H-axis relational space without requiring
    V-axis seniority structure. A junior can amae toward a senior or a peer.
W4: Can H-axis change without changing V-axis? → YES: a Japanese person can
    change job (H-axis institutional membership changes) while sempai-kōhai
    relations from university remain (V-axis unchanged). H and V are separable.
W5 (Root): H-axis in Japan is operationally independent — changes in present-moment
    relational state do not automatically change transgenerational position.
```

**ES Tag:** `[ES-PRATY]` — Doi (1973) amae; `[ES-PRATY]` — Lebra (1976) behavioral modes
**Operational independence test:** H-axis ⊥ V-axis = "changing state of one does not force change in the other." The job-change/sempai example demonstrates this operationally.

### R3 — Root: Does Japan confirm V ⊥ H as structural, or only as two named concepts?

**Finding: PARTIAL — operational orthogonality confirmed; formal mathematical proof not available from Nakane**

```
W1: What would "structural orthogonality" require? → V ⊥ H (mathematically) means:
    knowing V-axis state gives zero information about H-axis state, and vice versa.
    Alternatively: an entity can change maximally along one axis without moving
    along the other.
W2: Does Nakane's tate/yoko satisfy this? → Nakane claims tate and yoko are
    "analytically independent" organizing principles (Ch.1). Not derived from
    a formal model — empirical observation about Japanese social structure.
W3: Does the ie case satisfy H→V direction? → When all ie members die (H-axis
    state = 0 current members), the ie obligation still exists (V-axis state ≠ 0).
    This demonstrates V ≠ f(H) — V-axis is not a function of H-axis state.
W4: Does the Plath case satisfy V→H direction? → Plath (1964): the dead (V-axis
    presence) do not determine who the living interact with today (H-axis present
    relations). V-axis presence ≠ H-axis determined. V→H direction satisfied.
W5 (Root): Japan demonstrates operational V ⊥ H — changing one axis does not force
    change in the other (both directions). What is absent is a formal mathematical
    derivation. Sociological evidence is consistent with V ⊥ H but does not
    constitute a logical proof.
```

**Gap remaining:** Nakane's tate/yoko is a **sociological description**; Tiên Đề III's V ⊥ H is an **ontological claim**. Bridge argument in §4 below.

## 4. Bridge Argument — Sociological → Ontological

**The gap:** Nakane's tate/yoko is sociological. Tiên Đề III's V ⊥ H is ontological.

**Bridge (3 steps):**

```
Step 1 — Operational definition of orthogonality:
  V ⊥ H (operationally) = "changing state of one axis does not force change in
  the other axis." Weaker than mathematical orthogonality but sufficient for
  a structural claim.

Step 2 — Japan satisfies operational orthogonality:
  (a) H→V direction: ie persists when all H-axis members replaced (R3 W3)
  (b) V→H direction: dead (V-axis) present does not determine living (H-axis)
      present relations (R3 W4 via Plath 1964)
  → Both directions satisfied → operational V ⊥ H confirmed.

Step 3 — Ontological elevation:
  If V and H are operationally independent across ≥2 culturally distinct identity
  systems (Vietnam + Japan), and both systems arrived at this structure via
  different cultural paths (ancestor worship vs. ie/ba system), then V ⊥ H is
  more likely a structural property of relational identity systems generally,
  not a Vietnamese cultural artifact.
  ES tag: [ES-ANUMA] — valid inference, not proof.
```

**What the bridge does NOT establish:**
- Mathematical orthogonality (inner product = 0 in a formal vector space)
- Universality across all identity systems
- That V ⊥ H is *necessary* rather than *contingent*

**Tag:** `[ES-ANUMA]` `[proposed-by-this-project]`
